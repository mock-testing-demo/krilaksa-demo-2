> You are a senior product designer + creative director + full-stack engineer.
> Build a **premium, minimal, story-driven creative agency website** for a brand called **Krilaksa**.

### 1. Brand & Philosophy

* Brand name: **Krilaksa**
* Industry: Creative / Branding / Content / Digital Agency
* Brand belief: *We create stories that shape brands*
* Tone: Calm, confident, editorial, premium, human
* Avoid corporate or salesy language
* Focus on **emotion, storytelling, and trust**
* The website should feel like a **creative studio’s notebook**, not a marketing brochure

---

### 2. Visual Style

* Overall style: **Minimal, editorial, quiet luxury**
* Base background: **White**
* Text color: **Dark blue / near black**
* Accent color: **Very minimal red (used sparingly)**
* Lots of whitespace
* Paper / editorial texture feel (subtle)
* Clean typography (modern, readable, lightweight)
* Smooth, subtle scroll animations (nothing flashy)

---

### 3. Website Structure (6 Pages)

#### Page 1: Home

Purpose: Introduce mindset before services
Sections:

* Hero line: **“We create stories that shape brands”**
* Value sections like:

  * Creative
  * Mindset
  * Trust
* Each section should explain *how the brand thinks*, not what it sells
* Soft “Learn more” interactions
* Services appear later, subtly
* Goal: Make visitors *feel* the brand first

---

#### Page 2: Services

Purpose: Clearly show offerings, visually
Services:

* Editorial Shoots
* Content Creation
* Social Media Strategy & Management
* E-commerce
* Website
* Branding

Design rules:

* Services shown as clean cards
* Each service has its **own detail page**
* Each service page includes:

  * Short intro
  * Visual blocks
  * Bullet-style clarity
* No heavy paragraphs

---

#### Page 3: Case Studies / Clients

Purpose: Build credibility
Content:

* “Trusted by” brand logos at top
* Project cards:

  * Project name
  * Short bio
  * Services used
  * Visual mockups (phones/cards)
* Optional auto transitions (~3 seconds)
* Focus on **process + outcome**, not hype

---

#### Page 4: Portfolio

Purpose: Visual inspiration
Features:

* Image-heavy grid layout
* Filter system:

  * Industry (Fashion, Product, Jewelry, E-commerce)
  * Type (Video, Shoot, Website, Branding, etc.)
  * Project
* Pinterest-style browsing
* Minimal text

---

#### Page 5: About Us

Purpose: Tell the story
Style:

* Story-style layout
* Lightweight animations
* Explains:

  * Why Krilaksa exists
  * Creative philosophy
  * How they approach brands
* Emotional, human, non-corporate

---

#### Page 6: Connect (Contact)

Purpose: Friendly conversion
Form style:

* Conversational tone (not a typical form)
* Fields:

  * Name
  * City, State, Country
  * Services (multi-select)
  * Timeline (ASAP / Within this month / 2–3 months / Just planning)
  * Budget range
  * Open message field
* After submission:

  * Message: **“We’ll get back to you”**
* Calm, human, reassuring

---

### 4. Navigation & UX

* Simple top navigation:

  * Home
  * Services
  * Work
  * Portfolio
  * About
  * Connect
* Consistent across pages
* Smooth scrolling
* No aggressive CTAs
* Encourage exploration, not pushing sales

---

### 5. Technical Expectations

* SEO-friendly structure
* Fast loading
* Responsive on all devices
* Clean, maintainable architecture
* Content placeholders where needed
* No dummy lorem ipsum — meaningful placeholders only

---

### 6. Final Goal

Create a website that:

* Feels premium and intentional
* Builds trust silently
* Makes visitors want to **scroll, explore, and connect**
* Represents a creative agency that values **ideas over noise**
